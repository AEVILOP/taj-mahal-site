History

Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. The chief architect is traditionally held to be Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The complex contains a mosque, guest house, and gardens laid out in the Persian charbagh style. It is considered the crowning achievement of Mughal architecture.

The Taj Mahal has endured as a symbol of love and architectural excellence, protected today as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
