Overview

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum complex in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. Built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to immortalize Mumtaz Mahal, it combines Persian, Islamic and Indian architectural styles.

Designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983, it attracts millions of visitors and is one of India's most iconic monuments.

Location

Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

Built

1632–1653 (Mughal era)

Style

Mughal architecture — marble inlay & charbagh gardens
