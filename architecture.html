Architecture

The Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, noted for its symmetry, intricate marble inlay work, and balanced gardens. The central dome and four minarets, plus detailed pietra dura (stone inlay) illustrate the fusion of Persian, Ottoman Turkish and Indian influences.

The charbagh garden layout and water channels give the site a sense of order and reflection, symbolizing paradise. The mausoleum itself uses white marble with inlaid semi-precious stones forming floral patterns and calligraphy.

Key features
  • • Symmetry & axial planning
  • • Central bulbous dome
  • • Four slender minarets
  • • Marble pietra dura inlays
